## About

Hey, I'm memN0ps

I'm exploring UEFI programming and hypervisor development in my spare time. For fun and learning, I write experimental and unsafe Rust code to benefit the open-source community. It's important to note that my projects assume a certain level of knowledge in the respective subject matter and may require modifications for proper functionality. I intentionally make some projects challenging to discourage blind copying and emphasize understanding. 

Due to personal constraints, I avoid creating stable, publicly supported projects requiring ongoing maintenance. While my projects primarily serve as proofs-of-concept, they can be adapted for red teaming or game hacking with appropriate modifications. While C was my first language, I ultimately focused on Rust after exploring various programming languages. However, you can use any programming language. Once you understand the underlying techniques, implementing them in another language becomes straightforward. 

All of my code is licensed under the [MIT license](https://opensource.org/license/mit/). Please refrain from contacting me for help bypassing anti-cheats, AVs, EDRs, bug fixes, issue resolutions, or OPSEC-related matters. Commenting, opening issues, and creating pull requests are permanently disabled. Please handle everything independently. Proper attribution is appreciated if you choose to use my code; otherwise, kindly abstain from using it.

During my university years as a computer science student, I began delving into the realm of game hacking, which eventually sparked my interest in information security. Over the past eight years, I have explored various tools and techniques encompassing hacking, coding, reverse engineering and software exploitation, as shown by my experience, certifications, trainings, blog posts and projects. However, it is only recently that I have managed to carve out time for pursuing projects like developing a [Windows kernel rootkit](https://github.com/memN0ps/rootkit-rs), [UEFI bootkit](https://github.com/memN0ps/bootkit-rs), and a [blue-pill hypervisor](https://github.com/memN0ps/hypervisor-rs). My projects have helped me clarify my path and solidify my aspirations. As a result, I am more determined than ever to delve deeper into areas such as low-level programming in Rust, malware, hypervisors, Windows internals, reverse engineering, and Windows kernel exploitation and further explore the intricacies of game hacking techniques. I am excited to continue my journey and uncover new knowledge in these domains. With an unwavering dedication to security research, I am continuously driven to push the boundaries of knowledge in this field.

So far, my journey has encompassed many explorations without specializing in any particular area. Although, I am now eagerly seeking a niche where I can channel my passion and expertise. However, I am still a novice in many aspects and consider myself a perpetual learner.
